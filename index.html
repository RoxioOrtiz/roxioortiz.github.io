The Definitive Album Ranking (Swiftie Consensus)

It's impossible to choose, but if we *had* to... this highly subjective, yet totally accurate, list ranks the original studio albums from best to still-perfect. We love them all, *objectively*.

  1. Folklore - The surprise indie-folk masterpiece that showcased her unparalleled lyrical genius.
  2. 1989 - The flawless pop transition that broke every record and solidified her global superstardom.
  3. Red (Taylor's Version) - A chaotic masterpiece that captures the agony of heartbreak, now fully realized with "All Too Well (10 Minute Version)".
  4. Speak Now (Taylor's Version) - Written entirely by Taylor, a whimsical and heartfelt bridge from country to pop.
  5. Evermore - Folklore's introspective, cozy, and slightly darker sister album.
  6. Midnights - A collection of stories written in the middle of the night, full of catchy, synth-pop gems.
  7. Reputation - The defiant, electro-pop response to public scrutiny, a dark and powerful statement.
  8. Lover - A bright, romantic, and eclectic celebration of love in all its forms.
  9. Fearless (Taylor's Version) - The iconic, Grammy-winning sophomore album that made her a household name.
  10. The Tortured Poets Department - The newest chapter, a raw and poetic look at grief and complexity.
  11. Taylor Swift (Debut) - The charming country start that introduced the world to a young, gifted songwriter.

Swiftie Fun Facts You Should Know

A few essential tidbits to boost your Swiftie knowledge:

  • Lucky Number 13: Taylor considers 13 her lucky number. She was born on the 13th, turned 13 on a Friday the 13th, and used to draw the number on her hand before shows.
  • Christmas Tree Farm Roots: She grew up on a Christmas tree farm in Pennsylvania, which inspired her holiday song "Christmas Tree Farm."
  • Four-Time AOTY Winner: Taylor is the only artist to win the Grammy for Album of the Year four times (for *Fearless*, *1989*, *Folklore*, and *Midnights*).
  • Her Cats' Names: Her three beloved cats are named Meredith Grey, Olivia Benson, and Benjamin Button, all after famous TV/film characters.
